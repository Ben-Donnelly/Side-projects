This is what first sparked my interest in computer science. I had just finished a maths grind when I was 15 and I told my tutor that I had a hard time concentrating for such a long time.
He told me of this "Cognitive concentration" game that will help to improve my focus. He explained that the idea was to have a grid (start out with a 4x4) and randomly
have put the numbers 1-16 in them. The objective was to cross off the numbers in order and time yourself. If you manage to get a time below the goal
(he gave me a goal of 20 seconds at the time), then you increase the grid size by 1. "Keep doing this until you cant get below 20 seconds and then your goal is 25 seconds, always aim for 20".
I asked him how would I make the grids because I would know where the numbers are. He said to ask my dad and he could do them or
"There are programs online you could use" he said, in passing, as he was walking out. This kept me up late that thinking "How could a computer do that?", it was alien to me and I wanted to know more about computers and how they work.
